## TL;DR

US news aggregator app. **#1 most-downloaded news app in the US, ~50M MAU, $151–$182M raised, 2021 Series C valuation $1B (Francisco Partners $115M).** Currently positioning as ad-tech infrastructure (ClearStream).

Exposed by **Reuters investigation (June 5, 2024)**. Published fictitious "Christmas Day tragedy strikes Bridgeton, NJ" story; Bridgeton police rebutted on Facebook: "It is entirely false. Nothing even similar to this story occurred." A Colorado food bank turned people away after wrong food-distribution times; a Pennsylvania charity (Harvest912) complained that NewsBreak's false articles meant "homeless people will walk to these venues to attend a clinic that is not happening." **Reuters found 40+ such instances.**

Internal memo from consultant Norman Pearlstine to CEO Jeff Zheng May 2022: **"I cannot think of a faster way to destroy the NewsBreak brand."**

## Why it matters for Pier and Point

The aggregator-failure case. **~0% original content** + scaled AI processing = the [[human-moat-pattern|aggregation-only quadrant]] that didn't survive. Validates the compass artifact's claim that aggregators with no original presence (NewsBreak, SmartNews, Particle, Artifact) are structurally fragile compared to disclosed AI-assisted operators with original reporting (Citymeetings.nyc, 6AM City core markets, Block Club Chicago).

The **$1.75M Patch settlement** (separate ad-tech / content matter) is also worth knowing about as a defamation/false-content liability marker.
