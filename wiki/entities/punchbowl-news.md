## TL;DR

Capitol Hill power-dynamics newsletter. Niche-disciplined, premium-subscription operator. **$10M revenue year one (2021) → ~$20M by 2023. ~$1M initial founder funding. ~40 FTE today. Subscription revenue grew 60% YoY in H1 2025. Converted ~3,000 of 100,000 readers to paid early on at $300–$350/year.**

The compass artifact's **cleanest capital-efficiency exemplar**. $1M founder capital → $20M revenue in ~3 years, no venture funding required.

URL: <https://punchbowl.news>

## What it is, what it isn't

**It is**: niche-disciplined ("call balls and strikes" non-partisan stance, hard editorial focus on Capitol Hill), founder-led personality brand (Anna Palmer, Jake Sherman), subscription-revenue dominant, custom "Portal" subscriber product on Memberful billing.
**It isn't**: hyperlocal, generalist, or AI-driven. The tech stack is undisclosed.

## Why it matters for Pier and Point

Two specific lifts:

1. **Niche discipline**. Capitol Hill is a tighter niche than "Washington DC" or "national politics." Pier and Point's "Ventura County civic" must stay equally disciplined — broadening to "Southern California news" prematurely is the most common failure mode the compass artifact catalogs.
2. **Founder-led personality brand**. Punchbowl's success runs on Palmer/Sherman as recognizable individual voices. The [[human-moat-pattern|human moat]] applies regardless of beat — Finn-as-founder is not optional.
