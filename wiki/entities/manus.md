# Manus

Autonomous agent platform ([manus.im](https://manus.im)) — one of the cohort of 2026 general-purpose agent products that compete in the same space as [[claude-code]] and [[openclaw]]. Mentioned by [[greg-isenberg|Isenberg]] multiple times in [[isenberg-future-of-saas-30-step]] as one of the tools to use for both workflow extraction (Step 2) and content automation (Step 6).

## Where it sits in Isenberg's framing

Isenberg lists three interchangeable tools for the agentic workflow research / content / automation work: **Manus, Claude Code, ChatGPT**. He also throws in [[openclaw|OpenClaw]] / Cowork as honorable mentions. The implication: tool choice is less important than the agent-orchestration habit.

## Notes for Finn

Not currently in the [[claude-code|Claude Code]] / [[openclaw]] stack the wiki documents. If exploring Isenberg's content sub-loop ([[media-first-distribution]]), worth a one-day spike to compare Manus vs. running the same content engine in Cowork — particularly because Manus emphasizes long-running autonomous tasks, which is closer to what daily content scheduling needs.
