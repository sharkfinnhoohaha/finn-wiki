## TL;DR

Defunct AI-content-farm news operation. Cleanest cautionary tale in the [[ai-news-failures-curriculum|failure curriculum]]. Founder **Gurbaksh Chahal** (sold BlueLithium to Yahoo for $300M in 2007; convicted 2014 misdemeanor domestic violence battery) underpaid journalists in Iraq and India to feed competitor articles into his "ePiphany AI" tool. **About 25% of 1,000+ reviewed BNN stories were lifted from Reuters, AP, BBC.** BNN's "Geeta Pillai" byline carried **48,623 articles in 5.5 months — 291/day average**. The BNN Correspondents account hit **274,276 articles in 18 months**.

Exposed by **NYT investigation (June 6, 2024)**. Critically, BNN published a fabricated story claiming Irish broadcaster Dave Fanning faced trial for sexual misconduct — **Microsoft syndicated it to MSN's homepage; Fanning sued both BNN and Microsoft.** BNN shut down April 2024, briefly relaunched as Trimfeed, then went offline.

## Why it matters for Pier and Point

The Microsoft / Fanning lawsuit is the load-bearing precedent: **syndicating AI-generated content attaches publisher liability to the syndicator, not just the originator.** Anyone who runs Pier and Point articles through a third-party distribution surface (MSN, Apple News, Google News partner program) inherits the editorial responsibility.

The 291-articles-per-day Pillai byline is the volume mark above which any operation is incompatible with credible disclosure.
