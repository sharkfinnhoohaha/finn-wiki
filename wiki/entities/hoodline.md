## TL;DR

Hyperlocal news brand operating across ~25 cities. **The cautionary case proving exposure does not always kill an operation if ad revenue and search traffic hold.** Used 3–6 AI personas per city ("Nina Singh-Hudson", "Leticia Ruiz", "Eric Tanaka", "Jake Rodriguez") with AI-generated headshots. Exposed by Gazetteer SF + Bloomberg in March 2024. Pangram Labs CEO Max Spero: "straight-up AI spam." Nieman Lab's Neel Dhanesha raised "digital blackface" concerns about fake POC bylines assigned to demographically incongruous cities. **CEO Zack Chen requested Wayback Machine remove archived screenshots; Internet Archive's Mark Graham confirmed.** Hoodline switched from fake names to an "AI" badge per article + a public methods page — and continued operating.

The News Literacy Project called the post-exposure disclosure pattern "an empty gesture." Volume: 5–20 stories/day per city across ~25 cities. Revenue undisclosed; ~$10M VC raised pre-Impress3 acquisition (October 2020). Funded by ad revenue post-scandal.

## Stack

Proprietary platform "Atlas," historically built on Automated Insights' Wordsmith templating engine.

## Why it matters for Pier and Point

The negative reference. Specifically:

- **Hoodline's "AI" badge alone is not the disclosure pattern that survives.** [[transparency-premium-as-traffic-strategy|The defensible pattern]] is per-article block + permanent public methods page + named human editor + corrections page.
- **Exposure didn't kill them, but it disqualified them from grant pipelines, reputable journalism partnerships, and editor recruitment**. Pier and Point isn't going to outrun NewsGuard if the venture drifts toward AI-persona territory.
- **Volume threshold matters**: 5–20 stories/day × 25 cities = volume zone where penalty risk dominates. Pier and Point at 3–10 pieces/week in one market is well below this line by design.
