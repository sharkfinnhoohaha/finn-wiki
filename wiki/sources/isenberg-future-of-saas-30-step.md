# Greg Isenberg — SaaS is minting millionaires again (30-step playbook)

A monologue-style YouTube episode from [[greg-isenberg]]'s Startup Ideas podcast laying out a 30-step framework for building a niche, agent-fulfilled SaaS in 2026. Title on YouTube: *"SaaS is minting millionaires again (here's how)."* Captured 2026-04-25 via auto-caption transcript.

## TL;DR

SaaS isn't dying — it's morphing into **agent-fulfilled software for a subniche of a big market, sold per task, distributed by a media engine the founder also runs.** The end state Isenberg names is **"the default execution layer for that subniche"** — a piece of software that doesn't just *track* a workflow, it *runs* it.

The playbook compresses three habits Finn already has on file ([[productized-services]], [[wat-framework-marketing]], [[ninety-day-launch-plan]]) and adds three new tactical levers: **services-first sequencing** (start as a human-fulfilled service, then automate), **per-seat → per-task → outcome pricing** evolution, and **media-as-product** (content engine isn't marketing — it's part of the product).

## The 30 steps, condensed

Grouped into the four phases Isenberg implies but doesn't name explicitly.

### Phase 1 — Find the wedge (steps 1–5)

1. **Pick a subniche inside a big market.** "FIRE within finance," not "finance." Tools: [[ideabrowser]], LLMs.
2. **Map the subniche's workflow end-to-end.** Manually interview, do it yourself, or have an agent (Manus / [[claude-code]] / ChatGPT) extract it.
3. **Identify where money changes hands.** That's the wedge. (Sketch on FigJam, Excalidraw, paper — doesn't matter.)
4. **Spot repetitive mechanical steps.** That's where the agent goes.
5. **Quantify the cost of those steps.** "Save the owner 10 min/day at $400/hr time = $X/yr saved." Concrete number anchors pricing.

### Phase 2 — Build distribution before product (steps 6–10)

6. **Create scroll-stopping content around that workflow.** Pick one channel (IG / TikTok / X). Use AI as a CMO — research viral patterns in the niche, generate non-obvious ideas, build a calendar.
7. **Study posts that get saves, replies, DMs.** Develop intuition.
8. **Double down on organic angles that convert.** Find the few that work.
9. **Run paid ads on the proven organic winners.** Organic hits ≈ predictable paid ROI.
10. **Capture emails from day one.** The list is the foundation when paid and organic both fail.

### Phase 3 — Build the agent (steps 11–18)

11. **Manually perform the workflow.** Be the human service first. Most agent-SaaS starts as agencies.
12. **Document every step precisely.**
13. **Separate judgment from mechanical tasks.** AI is great at mechanical, mixed at judgment.
14. **Turn mechanical tasks into agent workflows.**
15. **Design agents to complete the full task** (not just suggest).
16. **Connect agents to real tools.** [[mcp-protocol|MCP]] is the wire — email, Slack, CRM, Stripe, niche-specific tools.
17. **Add orchestration, retries, and verifications** (with human-in-the-loop). Quotes Scott Belsky: *"the orchestration layer is the new interface layer."* See [[orchestration-as-interface]].
18. **Store user preferences and long-term memory.** Personalization is the moat.

### Phase 4 — Compound (steps 19–30)

19. **Launch narrow with high-touch onboarding.** Get rich data into the product immediately. Don't optimize signup-flow elegance; optimize signal capture.
20. **Publish measurable proof.** Hours saved, revenue moved, time returned.
21. **Move pricing from per-seat to per-task.** Public-market SaaS is down 30–50% partly because per-seat is dying.
22. **Shift to outcome pricing.** "$200 every time the workflow completes."
23. **Increase pricing as value compounds.** Brand trust + workflow depth = price elasticity.
24. *(Implicit step — buy or build adjacent.)* Add adjacent workflows once primary LTV is maxed.
25. **Orchestrate multiple agents across the lifecycle.**
26. **Build switching costs through data and memory.**
27. **Turn power users into public case studies.** Camera crew, not just a tweet. Then put paid spend behind the case study.
28. **Hire operators from inside the niche.** With profits, hire people who already know the work.
29. **Reinvest profits into distribution and product depth.** Both. Not either.
30. **Become the default execution layer for that subniche.** End state.

## What's new vs. prior wiki knowledge

The vault already has the "productize an AI service" thesis from [[ai-agency-niches]] / [[productized-services]] / [[deep-research-strategic-arbitrage|the Deep Research briefing]]. This source adds three lenses that weren't sharp before:

1. **Services-first sequencing** — every prior source treats the SaaS as the starting point. Isenberg's Step 11 says start as a service, with you manually running it. Captured as [[services-first-saas]].
2. **Per-task / outcome pricing as a deliberate evolution** — [[pricing-and-rates]] currently has only retainer + variable. Per-seat SaaS ≠ per-task agent execution; they price differently. Captured as [[per-task-pricing]].
3. **Media-as-product** — both [[wat-framework-marketing]] and [[ai-agency-niches]] mention content vaguely. Isenberg names it as half the moat, alongside the agent. Captured as [[media-first-distribution]].

The "orchestration is the new interface layer" idea (from [[scott-belsky]]) gets its own page because it reframes what an agent product *is*: not a UI on top of automation, but a coordinator on top of agent + human + tool. Captured as [[orchestration-as-interface]].

## Source-quality caveat

> [!warning] Founder-content energy, light on receipts
> Isenberg cites his own track record (advised TikTok and Reddit, sold three venture-backed companies) and one Scott Belsky tweet. No customer case studies, no revenue data, no failed-attempt counterexamples. The framework reads as well-considered pattern recognition from someone who watches a lot of solo founders, not as a documented playbook with controlled outcomes. Treat the steps as a checklist of things to consider — particularly the per-task pricing claim and the "default execution layer" end state — not as evidence any single business has hit step 30 by following it linearly.
