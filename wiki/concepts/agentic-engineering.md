# Agentic engineering

Karpathy's name for the discipline that sits above [[vibe-coding]]. Vibe coding raises the floor — anyone can ship something. Agentic engineering preserves the ceiling — the security, robustness, and quality bar professionals maintained before agents existed, while moving much faster than they did.

> "[16:15] Vibe coding is about raising the floor for everyone in terms of what they can do in software. But then I would say agentic engineering is about preserving the quality bar of what existed before in professional software." — Karpathy, Sequoia AI Ascent 2026

This is the page the studio's positioning hangs on. Overlook isn't selling vibe-coded sites — that's the floor, and the floor is everywhere. Overlook is selling the discipline that keeps the bar where it was, with one operator hitting throughput a small team used to need. See [[agentic-services-positioning]] and [[overlook-strategy-positioning]].

The 10x claim from the old engineering literature is now the wrong number. Karpathy:

> "[16:50] People used to talk about the 10x engineer previously. I think that this is magnified a lot more. 10x is not the speedup you gain. People who are very good at this peak a lot more than 10x." — Karpathy

The human's job migrates. Not typing, not implementing — spec, oversight, taste.

> "[20:34] You have to work with your agent to design a spec that is very detailed and maybe it's basically the docs and then get the agents to write them, and you're in charge of the oversight and the top level categories, but the agents are doing a lot of the under the hood." — Karpathy

> "[21:37] You're in charge of the taste, the engineering, the design… the engineers are doing the fill in the blanks." — Karpathy

The hiring rubric flips with it. Stop giving puzzle interviews. Give a real project and watch someone defend it.

> "[18:46] Hiring has to look like give me a really big project and see someone implement that big project. Like let's write say a Twitter clone for agents and then make it really good, make it really secure… I'm going to use 10 Codex 5.4x for X-high to try to break your website that you deployed." — Karpathy

That's the operating model the studio sells inward and outward: spec-driven dev, taste as the human role, an audit step where adversarial agents try to break what was built. See [[workflow-audit-service]], [[understanding-as-bottleneck]], [[skills-as-primitive]], [[jaggedness-and-verifiability]] for the discipline's components — what to build skills for, where the model will hold up, what only the human can do. The threshold this discipline assumes was crossed in late 2025 — see [[agentic-inflection-late-2025]].
