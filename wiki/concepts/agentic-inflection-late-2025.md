# Agentic inflection late 2025

The threshold both Karpathy and Howie Liu point at, in different vocabularies, with the same date in mind. Late 2025 is when agents stopped being kind-of-helpful and started being autonomously trusted. This is the date that matters for any client positioning — the line where "we tried AI" stops being a defensible reason to stay on the sidelines.

Karpathy: December 2025 was the personal inflection.

> "[1:16] December was this clear point where for me… the chunks just came out fine and then I kept asking for more and it just came out fine and then I can't remember the last time I corrected it, and then I just trusted the system more and more and then I was vibe coding." — Karpathy, Sequoia AI Ascent 2026

> "[1:47] A lot of people experienced AI last year as a ChatGPT-adjacent thing. But you really had to look again as of December because things have changed fundamentally and especially on this agentic coherent workflow that really started to actually work." — Karpathy

Howie Liu: Opus 4.5, around November 2025, was the autonomy threshold for shipped engineering work.

> "[5:16] Opus 4.5 just kind of set a new high water mark of like, wo, this thing for the first time really feels like a true software engineer that's able to work on a task that would have taken a real human engineer maybe many hours if not days. It can go do it completely autonomously and it ships me a perfect clean PR that I can just review like a reviewer would." — Howie Liu

Same inflection. Different vocabularies. Karpathy frames it as trust ("I stopped correcting them"); Howie frames it as autonomy duration ("multi-hour to multi-day tasks"). Both are pointing at the same crossover from copilot to autopilot — the line below which an agent assists you, and above which it ships work you sign off as a reviewer.

For Overlook this is the date the pitch hangs on. Two operating implications:

1. **Objection handling.** Any prospect who says "we tried AI last year" tried it on the wrong side of this line. The honest reframe: the tools that worked in 2025-Q1 are not the tools that work in 2026-Q2. See [[agentic-engineering]] for the discipline that became viable after the threshold.

2. **Urgency math.** The interval between the inflection (late 2025) and broad enterprise adoption is the studio's window. Howie's framing — "many companies are barely catching up to the three-year-ago state-of-the-art let alone disrupting themselves with the new state-of-the-art" — describes the gap. The studio sells the gap. See [[agentic-services-positioning]] and [[deep-research-strategic-arbitrage]].

The frontmatter date on this page (`2026-04-29`) is roughly five months past the inflection. That puts the studio in the sweet spot — late enough that the threshold is durable, early enough that very few clients have internalized it. Lead with the inflection, anchor the case for hiring the studio on it, and use it to break ties on every "should we wait" objection. See also [[vibe-coding]] for the floor-level mode that crossed this line, and [[software-3-0]] for the worldview the inflection enables.
