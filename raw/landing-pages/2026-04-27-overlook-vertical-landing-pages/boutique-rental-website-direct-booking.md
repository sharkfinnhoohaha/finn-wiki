# A Direct-Booking Site That Ends the Airbnb Tax on Your Property

A custom Next.js website for owner-operated boutique rentals — fast, photo-led, and built so guests find you on Google instead of paying a 17% platform fee on top of your nightly rate.

## The Problem with Renting on Platforms Alone

Airbnb and Vrbo gave you the bookings to start the business and now own the customer relationship. Every night you accept through them is a night where the guest's loyalty belongs to the platform, the search rankings belong to the platform, and roughly a sixth of your revenue belongs to the platform. Repeat guests still book through Airbnb out of muscle memory. Your only differentiator is the property itself, fighting for attention against ten thousand other listings on the same map.

## My Approach

I build the property a real home on the open web — Next.js for speed, Sanity for the listing details and seasonal content, Vercel for global edge delivery, and a direct booking integration with whichever PMS you already use (OwnerRez, Hostaway, Lodgify, Smoobu — your choice). The site ranks for "[neighborhood] boutique rental" and for your property name, captures the email, and gives returning guests a one-click way to book direct without ever opening Airbnb again.

## The Outcome

The platform stays as a top-of-funnel channel; it stops being the entire funnel. Owners I've worked with see a meaningful share of nights shift from platform to direct within six months — the math on that shift is straightforward, since every direct night recovers the host fee and the credit card surcharge. More importantly, you own the guest list. Newsletter, returning-guest discounts, off-season offers — all possible the day after launch.

## Recent Work — Sømliøya, Norway

For Sømliøya — a boutique island rental in northern Norway — I built a single-property site that doubles as a destination guide. Sanity manages the photo galleries, seasonal availability notes, and the local-experience content that gives the property a story Airbnb cannot tell. Bookings flow through the owner's PMS via API; the site itself loads in under a second on a 4G phone. The same architecture works for one cabin in Ojai, three cottages on the Mendocino coast, or a six-unit boutique in Joshua Tree.

## FAQ

**Will I lose Airbnb visibility if I have my own site?**
No. The platform listing stays exactly as it is. The site is a parallel channel — most owners see Airbnb traffic stay flat while direct traffic grows alongside it.

**Which PMS do you integrate with?**
OwnerRez, Hostaway, Lodgify, Smoobu, Guesty, and Hospitable have native widgets I work with regularly. If you're on something custom, I write a small adapter — usually a day of work.

**How do guests find a brand-new site on Google?**
The first wave is direct: the QR code in the welcome book, the post-checkout email, the property's existing repeat guests. SEO compounds from there — most properties start ranking for "[town name] [property type]" within four to six months.

**Can I run the booking through Stripe directly and skip the PMS fee too?**
Yes, on the Standard and Showcase tiers. I can wire Stripe Checkout to a custom availability calendar — but only if you're disciplined about blocking dates across channels. Otherwise the PMS embed is safer.

**Do you take a cut of bookings?**
No. I'm paid for the build. Anything you make through the site after launch is yours.
