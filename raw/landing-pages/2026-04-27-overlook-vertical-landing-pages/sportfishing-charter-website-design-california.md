# A Charter Site That Books Trips Without the FishingBooker Cut

A direct-booking website for California sportfishing charters — built on Next.js and Sanity, fast on a phone in the parking lot, and structured to rank for "Channel Islands sportfishing" before the OTAs do.

## The Problem with Charter Sites Today

Most California charter operators run on a wordpress theme from a decade ago and a FishingBooker listing that takes 15 to 20 percent off every trip. The owner's own site loads slowly, the trip-types page is a wall of text, and the booking widget — if there is one — kicks the customer to a third-party domain at the worst possible moment. So the charter pays the platform fee on bookings the captain's reputation actually earned.

## My Approach

I treat the charter site as a real product page for a real boat. Trip types, bait packages, departure times, and weather-dependent variants live in Sanity. The booking flow stays on your domain via FareHarbor, Checkfront, or a direct Stripe integration — depending on your scale and how much custom logic your trips require. Every page is statically rendered, so a customer pulling up your site in the harbor parking lot at 4:45am gets the boat number and slip in under two seconds.

## The Outcome

The customer who used to find you through FishingBooker now finds you through Google, books on your domain, and saves you the platform fee. Your reputation compounds on a property you own. Captains I've talked to in this space estimate the fee differential alone pays the build back inside one season — and that's before counting the repeat-customer email list you couldn't build through the OTA.

## FAQ

**Can you replace FareHarbor or do I have to keep paying their fees?**
Either. Most charters keep FareHarbor for the dispatch and customer-management features and just embed it on a faster, better-designed site. If you want zero per-booking fees, I can wire a direct Stripe Checkout flow on the Standard tier — works best for smaller fleets with simple trip structures.

**How is this different from a $99 Squarespace template?**
Speed, schema, and ownership. Squarespace charter templates render slowly on cellular, lack proper schema for Google's "things to do" pack, and lock your content into their export format. Next.js + Sanity loads instantly, surfaces in the local activities pack, and gives you a portable codebase.

**Will the site work when the boat is full and I'm at sea?**
Yes. Sanity is cloud-based, the site is statically rendered on Vercel's edge, and your booking provider handles confirmations automatically. Nothing on the site needs you to be at a desk.

**How do I rank for "[my city] fishing charter" against FishingBooker?**
Local schema, Google Business Profile linkage, weekly fish-report posts (about 200 words each), and earning a few real backlinks from harbor associations and tackle shops. FishingBooker outranks generic charter sites — it does not consistently outrank a well-structured local operator over the long run.

**What if I just want a simple one-page site?**
The Foundation tier is essentially that — a polished single-page presence with a booking embed. Three weeks, $3,800, and you own everything.
