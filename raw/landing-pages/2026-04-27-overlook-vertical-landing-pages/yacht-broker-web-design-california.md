# A Brokerage Website That Sells the Boat Before You Pick Up the Phone

A photo-led, listing-ready site for California yacht brokers — built on Next.js and Sanity, deployed on Vercel, optimized for "Boats for Sale" queries that actually convert.

## The Problem with Most Yacht Brokerage Sites

Most California brokerage sites are stuck inside an MLS template from 2014 — pinched thumbnails, slow listing pages, branding that disappears the moment a buyer lands on the inventory grid. Buyers who can afford a $400K trawler do not tolerate slow image loads. They click back to YachtWorld, where every broker looks identical, and the lead never comes home to your domain.

## My Approach

I build the brokerage site as the trustworthy parent of your YachtWorld feed, not a copy of it. Listings sync from your existing MLS or CSV export into Sanity, render statically through Next.js (so a 12MP gallery still loads in under two seconds), and live alongside the broker bios, harbor photography, and survey-process pages that actually justify your commission. One CMS, one design system, one domain that ranks.

## The Outcome

A site that earns the inquiry on its own — not on the back of a paid YachtWorld upgrade. Clients have seen direct contact form submissions go from a trickle to a steady weekly stream within a quarter, and time-on-listing-page roughly double once the gallery and spec sheet stop fighting each other for space. The deliverable is a brokerage presence buyers screenshot and forward to their spouse before they ever call.

## FAQ

**Can you sync with YachtWorld?**
Yes. I import via the YachtWorld export feed into Sanity on a schedule. Listings on your domain update within an hour of any change in YachtWorld.

**How do I update listings without paying you for every change?**
Sanity Studio. Your broker or assistant edits price, photos, and status directly. I train one person on launch day and leave a video walkthrough.

**Will this rank above YachtWorld for my listings?**
Not for the boat name itself — YachtWorld's domain authority is too established. You will rank for "[your brokerage name]," "[city] yacht broker," and the long-tail listing pages that YachtWorld groups generically.

**Do I need professional photography?**
Yes. The biggest lift on most brokerage sites is replacing iPhone marina shots with a half-day of harbor photography. I don't shoot, but I can recommend two California marine photographers I trust.

**What if I sell the brokerage in two years?**
The site is yours. Sanity, Vercel, and the codebase all live in your accounts under your domain. I'm not the kind of agency that holds your CMS hostage.
