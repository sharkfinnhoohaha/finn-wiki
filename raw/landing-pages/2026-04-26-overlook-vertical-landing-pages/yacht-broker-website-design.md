# Yacht Broker Websites That Sell Listings, Not Just Display Them

A fast, broker-grade site with live MLS feeds, listing pages that load like a luxury catalog, and inquiry forms that route straight to your CRM.

## The problem with most yacht broker websites

Most broker sites are template builds running on PHP CMSes from 2014. Listings load slow, photos compress badly, the YachtWorld feed updates on a cron that breaks once a quarter, and the contact form ends up in a Gmail folder no one checks. Buyers who can drop $400k on a hull won't fight a 4-second load time to inquire — they bounce to the next broker on Google.

## My approach

I build yacht broker sites on Next.js 14 with Sanity for the content layer, deployed on Vercel's edge. Listings ingest from YachtWorld, YATCO, or Boats Group via their public feeds, get rebuilt on a webhook, and serve as static pages with full Lightroom-grade photo optimization. Inquiries route to HubSpot or Pipedrive with UTM data attached, so you know which listing and which channel produced the lead.

## What you get

A site that loads under 1.5s on cellular, ranks for "[your city] yacht broker" inside 90 days, and gives buyers a reason to inquire on your site instead of YachtWorld's. Photo galleries that match the boat's price point. A featured-listings system you can edit without calling me. Analytics wired up so you can see exactly which boats drive inquiries and which sit dead.

## Case study: Rustler Yachts

When I rebuilt Rustler Yachts' site, the brief was to make a 130-year-old British boatbuilder feel as considered online as their hulls feel in person. The result was a Next.js + Sanity build with a custom configurator, dealer locator, and editorial-grade owner stories. Inquiry volume on the spec pages roughly doubled in the first quarter post-launch, and the team can update model pages themselves without a developer in the loop.

## Process

1. **Discovery (week 1).** I review your current site, listings feed, CRM, and the three closest competing brokers in your region.
2. **Design (weeks 2–3).** Wireframes, then high-fidelity Figma mockups for home, listings index, single listing, and broker bio pages.

4. **Launch + tune (week 8).** DNS cutover, redirects from your old URLs, Search Console submission, and a 30-day SEO follow-up.

## FAQ

**Will the YachtWorld feed actually stay in sync?**
Yes. I rebuild the site on a webhook from your feed provider, and listings rebuild on a 15-minute cron as a backup. If a listing changes status, the site reflects it in under 20 minutes.

**Can I keep my current domain and email?**
Yes. I don't touch email DNS records unless you want me to migrate hosting. Domain stays in your registrar account, you stay in control.

**What if I sell my brokerage in two years?**
The site is yours — code in your GitHub, content in your Sanity org, deployed under your Vercel account. You can sell it, hand it to a new dev, or keep me on retainer.

**Do you handle photos?**
I optimize and crop what you have. For new shoots I'll point you to two California marine photographers I trust. I don't shoot myself.

**How is this different from a CharternetWeb or Relevance Yacht template?**
Those are good products if you want a $200/month rented site that looks like every other broker. I'm building you a custom asset on a modern stack, owned by you, that ranks better and converts better. The math works above roughly $1.5M in annual brokered volume.
