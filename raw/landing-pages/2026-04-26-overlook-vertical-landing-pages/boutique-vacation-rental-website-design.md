# Boutique Vacation Rental Websites That Drive Direct Bookings

Custom-designed sites with real booking integration, the photography to match your rate card, and the SEO to outrank your Airbnb listing for your own property name.

## The problem with most rental websites

Most owners end up with one of two things: a Lodgify or Hostfully template that looks identical to 50,000 other rentals, or a beautiful Squarespace site that can't actually take a booking. Neither one earns the 15% you're paying Airbnb. If a guest searches your property name and your Airbnb listing outranks your own site, the platform owns the relationship — and it's eating your margin every booking.

## My approach

I design boutique rental sites the way you'd design a small luxury hotel brand. Custom photography direction, a typography system that matches the property's actual feel, and a booking flow built on Hostfully, Guesty, OwnerRez, or a direct Stripe + iCal sync depending on your stack. The site is built on Next.js with Sanity for content, so adding a new property or season takes minutes, not a developer call.

## What you get

A site that wins your branded search, converts at 4–6% on direct traffic versus 1.5% on Airbnb, and gives returning guests a reason to book direct. Schema markup so Google shows your rates and availability. A guidebook system guests can use during their stay. Analytics wired up so you can see channel mix without logging into three dashboards.

## Case study: Sømliøya, Norway

Sømliøya is a private island rental in northern Norway with a strict aesthetic and a complicated booking calendar. I built the site as a slow, photography-led experience — the home page is essentially a film about the place — backed by a custom availability system tied to their PMS. The site has been the primary direct-booking channel since launch, with average session duration over 4 minutes and a return-visitor rate above 30%.

## Process

1. **Discovery (week 1).** Property tour (or full photo + video set), brand audit, current booking channel mix, and PMS review.
2. **Brand + design (weeks 2–4).** Logo refinement if needed, type and color system, then high-fidelity mockups for home, property, booking, and journal pages.
3. **Build (weeks 5–8).** Next.js development, Sanity schemas, PMS or booking integration, payment flow, guidebook system.
4. **Launch + SEO (weeks 9–10).** Migration plan, redirects, Search Console submission, schema validation, and a 60-day SEO follow-up.

## FAQ

**Won't I lose Airbnb's traffic?**
You'll keep your Airbnb listings — I'm not telling anyone to delist. But the math on direct booking is real: a site that captures even 30% of your repeat guests pays for itself inside a year on a $300/night property. Airbnb stays as the discovery channel.

**Can I take direct bookings without a PMS?**
Yes — for a single property, an iCal sync to Airbnb/VRBO plus Stripe payments is enough. PMS makes sense above 2 properties or above ~$200k in annual revenue.

**What about insurance and damage protection?**
I integrate with Safely, Waivo, or your PMS's built-in coverage. The site collects the deposit and waiver before booking confirmation.

**Do I need new photography?**
Probably. Boutique rental sites live or die on photography. I don't shoot, but I'll direct a shoot and connect you with photographers I've worked with — typical photo budget runs $1,500–$4,000 for a property.

**How is this different from Lodgify or Goliday?**
Those are SaaS products. They're fine if you want a templated site for $30/month and you don't care about ranking or branded experience. I'm building you a custom asset that you own, that looks like your property and not like everyone else's.
