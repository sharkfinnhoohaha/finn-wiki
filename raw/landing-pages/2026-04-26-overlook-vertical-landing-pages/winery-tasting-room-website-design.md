# Winery & Tasting Room Websites Built for Wine Club Growth

Custom-designed sites with reservation integration, DTC shipping, and a wine club flow that actually converts cellar-door visitors into members.

## The problem with most winery websites

Most Central Coast winery sites fall into two camps: a five-year-old Squarespace build with a Tock booking widget bolted on, or a Vin65/WineDirect template that handles ecommerce but looks like every other winery in Paso. Neither one captures the 12-month wine club conversion window after a tasting visit. If a guest leaves your tasting room without joining the club, you've got 30 days to win them back online — and a generic site doesn't.

## My approach

I build winery sites on Next.js with Sanity for content, integrated with WineDirect (formerly Vin65), Commerce7, or Offset for ecommerce and club management. Reservations route through Tock or SevenRooms with the calendar embedded properly, not popped out in an iframe. The brand work treats the site like an extension of the tasting room — the same paper, ink, and pacing — instead of a generic ecommerce shop with a wine bottle on it.

A site that ranks for "[your AVA] winery tasting room" inside 90 days, captures email addresses from guests before they leave the tasting room (QR-driven landing pages count), converts visitors to club members at 2–3x your current rate, and handles DTC shipping compliance for the 47 states you can ship to.

1. **Discovery (week 1).** Tasting room visit if you're in Santa Barbara, San Luis Obispo, or Ventura county; otherwise a remote brand audit. Review your current site, POS, club platform, and Tock/SevenRooms setup.
2. **Brand + design (weeks 2–4).** Type, color, and photography system. High-fidelity mockups for home, wines, club, visit, and journal pages.

## FAQ

**Will I have to leave WineDirect or Commerce7?**
No. I integrate with whatever you're already running. The site is a frontend on top of your existing club and shipping system, so your wine club, members, and SKUs stay in place.

**Can you handle DTC shipping compliance?**
Yes — I wire up the state-by-state logic through your ecommerce platform, but I'm not your compliance lawyer. For the actual licenses and bond filings, you'll work with ShipCompliant or your TTB attorney.

**How does the site help convert tasting room visitors to club members?**
QR-driven landing pages at the bar, a follow-up email sequence triggered the day after a reservation, and a club page designed to convert on mobile (because that's where they'll click the email). I track the full path so you see which of those moves is working.

**I'm in Paso Robles, not Santa Barbara — do you only work locally?**
Locally is faster, but I work with wineries anywhere in California and Oregon. The discovery visit is the only piece that benefits from in-person, and we can do that as a one-day trip.

**Why not just use the WineDirect storefront templates?**
They work, and they're cheap. If you're under $200k in DTC revenue, stay on a WineDirect template and don't pay me. Above that line, a custom site converts better, ranks better, and stops looking like every other winery in your AVA — and the math starts working.
